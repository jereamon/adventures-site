Redwoods and Northern California

This trip was an excuse to watch a disc golf tournament and play a legendary course. Along the way I played 24 different courses, did some hiking, and made a tentative friendship with a herd of elk.

Now, I live in Washington. So of course this trip didn't take place solely in California and I discovered some of my favorite disc golf courses are actually quite close to home.

My first stop was Wheatland disc golf course. Just north of Salem and right in the Willamette River flood plain. Fortunately the course happened to be in great condition. All dried out, recently mowed and ready to play.

Having taken highway 199 from Grant's Pass I quickly found myself in big tree territory. As much as I love redwoods I can't help but be a little disappointed by how few long hikes there are.

I'm not sure if I just don't know about them or if it's an indication of how few are left. There are still plenty of groves to explore and the Smith River has some great beaches.

My first glimpse of the Pacific Ocean at Crenscent City, CA.

I've been trying to travel as cheaply as possible. As such some nights are spent in less than desirable places. Other times you find the perfect sheltered spot right across the road from a gorgeous beach, and get to stay there for free.

So I had a good time wandering around the redwoods and played some fun courses along the way. You might be wondering where the photos of those courses are. The sad truth is I don't take photos while I'm disc golfing. It's a bad habit but one I haven't broken yet. Someday...

Eventually though I made it to San Francisco and did some exploring in Golden Gate Park.

I went to the California Academy of Science. Primarily for the planetarium, but I also happened to show up right when their butterflies were hatching. So I had to hang out in the jungle for a while trying to get a good close up shot of one.

I wandered into the tea garden and wound up there for quite a while. It's a beatiful space.

After a day in San Fran I was ready to get the hell out of there. So I made the drive down the coast to Santa Cruz and settled in to spectate the Steady Ed Memorial Master's Cup.

Two days played on the unforgiving DeLaveaga disc golf course where one small mistake will send you eighty feet down a steep hill into poison oak laden brush. And one day on the temporary course set up on the nearbye ball golf course. An overall longer course, although with more room for recovery.

The tournament was fun to watch but I was more than ready to get out of California by this point. The drive North flew by in a blur of courses and I played some of my favorites for a second time.

I found my way back home and, wanting to get out into woods, talked my parents into hiking into Falls Creek Falls.
