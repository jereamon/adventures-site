Olympic National Park

I wasn't sure what to expect with Olympic National Park. Well, that's not completely true. I do my research. But it seemed like such an obscure notion to combine rainforest, jagged crumbling mountains, and rugged remote coastline all in one park.

After a couple of soggy days disc golfing in the rain my stay in/near the park began at Lake Cushman. Located on the East side of the Olympic peninsula this massive lake is nestled in amongst Mt.Ellinor, Mt.Washington, and Copper Mountain.

The weather was still a little gray. A lot gray. Too gray for pretty landscape photos so I took photos of my less than pretty face. But as you can see. There's a wonderful body of water right behind my body.

This grey day was punctuated by light drizzle and the occasional lighter gray patch in the sky. My spirits weren't dampened thanks to the wonders of technology and the upcoming forecast.

Staying at Staircase Campground I found some excellent hiking along the North Fork of the Skokomish River, and into the surrounding mountains.

I found my way to Wagonwheel Lake. This trail was not an easy one. But hey, I was looking for a challenge. 2.8 miles one way with 3365 feet of elevation gain.

After an exhausting day I took the next off. Spending it at camp slacking around.

My day of rest felt well deserved but I didn't let myself stop for long. The next day was a transition from the East side of the park to the West. I played the wonderful Shelton Springs disc golf course a couple of times and drove over to Lake Quinault.

I spent some time in and around the Lake Quinault Lodge and walked the nearby nature trails. Being a nature trail, there were helpful signs along the way informing me of the way the plants and animals interact.

I'm not here to educate though. I merely provide some pretty pictures and minor locational details. If you want to learn about the Quinault Rainforest head out there and read all the signs for yourself.

Kalaloch beach. My final stop on this trip. I still don't know how to pronounce the name. An unforgiving and remote expanse of beautiful coastline. I found myself entranced and quite cold, with a constant chilly wind blowing in from the north.
