Central Cascades

I didn't know about the quality of adventuring to be had in the Central and North Cascades until last summer. Somehow I managed to remain unaware of the extraordinary sights and views for years.

I knew I had been to Leavenworth before. At some point. Maybe. But I had no idea the string of lakes known as the enchantments and the staggering granite peaks surrounding them existed at all. This revelation led me to quickly plan a trip to at least skim the surface of the extensive Alpine Lakes Wilderness.

I began my trip with a visit to the grandparents in Cle Elum and from there to Johnny Creek Campground. Merely twelve miles from Leavenworth this campground puts you in an excellent position for an early drive to the Stewart/Colchuck Lakes trailhead.

My first hike turned into a larger adventure than I had originally planned. Fortunately I started early and brought plenty of food, water, and advil. I found myself at Colchuck lake just before sunrise.

my plan was to hike into Colchuck and Stewart lake but when I arrived at Colchuck and found myself confronted by Aasgard Pass I knew I couldn't leave without attempting to climb it.

Aasgard pass is the low spot on the far left in this photo, with Dragontail Peak just to the right of it. I didn't realize at the time that it's around 2000 feet of elevation gain in a little under three quarters of a mile.

The way up the pass was tough, but mitigated by the fact that there were friendly mountain goats to guide the way. At the top I found them butting horns and casually laying in the snow as they shed their winter coat.

Of course getting to the top of Aasgard Pass wasn't quite enough for me and I soon found myself trekking further up a nearby hill that looked mellow compared to the nearby jagged peaks. I made my way up to a wonderful flat area and took in the view.

And obviously I had to lean over the edge of a huge cliff. What else are you going to do when you've just hiked your way up from 3400 feet to around 8000?

If you're wondering how I took that picture looking down on myself you'll have to keep wondering.

I hiked up a ways with these two guys on the way to Colchuck Lake. They were attempting a summit on Dragontail Peak. They're those two little dots in that massive snow field.

Having made it to the highest point that didn't require any technical climbing I began my descent. Looking down on Colchuck Lake I realized just how far I had to go.

What looks like a small body of water from this vantage had taken me close to 40 minutes to hike around that morning. Now I had 2000 vertical feet to lose to get to it plus about four miles of trail to the car once I'd circled the lake.

Well a couple hours later, at least I was down the pass. And as I looked back up towards it's top I couldn't help but think it looked drastically different than when I had first laid eyes upon it that morning.

To my painful inflamed knees it looked inexorably tall, an unreachable summit. But to the little part of my brain that never thought I would make it up in the first place it looked like a surmountable challenge. One I'd accomplished and planned to again already.

The very next day, on weary legs, I hiked into Eightmile Lake. I couldn't get enough of the area and wanted nothing more than to be back in the wilderness that had so captivated me the day before.

From ther my next stop was Lake Wenatchee. I made my way around the south side of the lake to Glacier View Campground. True to it's name I quickly found a lakefront site and picked out Glacier Peak peaking out from between Dirtyface Peak and Mt.David.

Having spent a relaxing day basking in the sun at Lake Wenatchee I set out for my final hike of this short trip. To the South of the lake is a long ridge. High up on this ridge at 6200 feet is the Alpine Lookout.

Having started early once again I found myself at the lookout right in time for a delicious breakfast of cold oats. I kicked back for a bit. Hiked down and began the disconsolate drive home. Saying goodbye to this area was tough. But I live close and will be back soon.
